Projects


Here, I showcase a variety of projects I've worked on, from coding and electronics to game development and beyond. With a passion for solving problems and exploring new technologies, I dive into each project with curiosity and a drive to push the boundaries of what’s possible.

Whether it’s building custom games, experimenting with the latest gadgets, or blending tech with fitness, I love creating things that challenge and inspire me. Take a look around to explore the projects that fuel my passion for continuous learning and growth!

Provided are two 'Gundam' designs, created in Blender by meticulously tracing over an existing drawing to produce two distinct meshes. Each mesh captures the intricate details and dynamic design of the original artwork while incorporating personalized touches to give them a fresh, stylized look.
The process involved careful attention to both the mechanical and organic aspects of the design, ensuring the models stay true to the iconic Gundam aesthetic while allowing for new creative elements.
The final result is a high-quality, fully detailed 3D representation of the Gundam universe, ready for further refinement or rendering.

This animation features a humanoid character wielding a greatsword, created for my game in Unreal Engine. The focus is on realistic weight distribution, capturing how the sword’s heft affects the character’s movement.
From the wind-up to the follow-through, the animation emphasizes the natural shift in the character’s posture, making each swing feel powerful and grounded, adding immersion and authenticity to the combat.

This animation features two humanoid characters engaged in a back-and-forth meele. Although not extensively long, it was my first attempt at animating reactions between more than one body.

A snippet of code produced in the Arduino IDE meant for capturing data transmitted by a compatible magnetometer. The data would be displayed in the serial monitor on the 9600 hertz frequency channel and could be plotted to showcase fluctuations in surrounding magnetic fields.
The code, unfortunately, doesn't interpret data in a way that is understandable by humans. (Not yet)

I developed a text compression script using a well-known algorithm to compress game terrain data, which was then serialized with JSON for real-time map loading.
The compression also unintentionally introduced a layer of obfuscation, enhancing data security and making it more resistant to tampering.
This solution optimized performance while adding an extra layer of protection to the game’s map data.
